## 5. Alert and Notification Systems

### 5.1 Alert System Limitations

The alert system may:
- **Fail to trigger** when conditions warrant an alert
- Generate **false positive alerts** (alerts when no actual issue exists)
- Generate **false negative alerts** (fail to alert when issues exist)
- Be **delayed** in notification delivery
- Fail to deliver notifications due to device settings, network issues, or app permissions

### 5.2 Critical Alert Types

Alerts for the following conditions are subject to limitations:

#### 5.2.1 Vital Signs Alerts
- May not trigger if sensor readings are inaccurate
- May trigger false alarms due to device movement or improper placement
- Threshold settings may not be appropriate for all individuals

#### 5.2.2 Panic/SOS Button Alerts
- Require the user to be able to physically press the button
- May fail if device is out of range or powered off
- Button may malfunction or be accidentally triggered

#### 5.2.3 Geofence Alerts
- Depend on GPS accuracy (see Section 7)
- May not trigger if device is offline or GPS is unavailable
- Boundary violations may not be detected in real-time

#### 5.2.4 Inactivity Alerts
- Based on motion sensor data which may be inaccurate
- Cannot distinguish between restful sleep and medical emergencies
- May not trigger if device is removed or not worn

#### 5.2.5 Device Offline Alerts
- May be delayed in detection
- Cannot alert if the monitoring platform itself is offline

#### 5.2.6 Dwell Time Alerts
- Based on location accuracy which may vary
- Cannot determine the reason for prolonged dwell time
- May not indicate an actual emergency

### 5.3 Notification Delivery

Notifications may fail to be delivered due to:
- Mobile device settings (Do Not Disturb mode, notification permissions)
- Network connectivity issues
- Email spam filters
- SMS/text message delivery failures
- Push notification service outages
- App being closed or force-stopped
- Device being in airplane mode or powered off

### 5.4 Response Time

**The Company does NOT guarantee:**
- Any specific response time for alerts
- That alerts will be received in real-time
- That alerts will reach all designated recipients
- That recipients will see or respond to alerts

### 5.5 User Responsibility for Alert Configuration

Users are responsible for:
- Properly configuring alert thresholds
- Ensuring appropriate recipients are designated
- Regularly testing alert functionality
- Maintaining active notification channels (email, phone, etc.)
- Ensuring notification permissions are enabled on their devices

**Improperly configured alerts may fail to trigger or may trigger unnecessarily.**

## 7. Location Tracking Services

### 7.1 GPS Accuracy Limitations

GPS tracking may be:
- **Inaccurate** by several meters to hundreds of meters
- Affected by atmospheric conditions, building structures, or tree cover
- Unavailable in indoor environments
- Degraded in urban canyons (tall buildings)
- Subject to satellite availability and signal quality

### 7.2 Indoor Tracking Limitations

Indoor tracking using floor plans and beacons:
- Depends on proper beacon placement and configuration
- May be inaccurate due to signal interference
- Requires uploaded and correctly scaled floor plans
- May not work in all indoor environments
- Can be affected by building materials (concrete, metal, etc.)

### 7.3 Geofencing Accuracy

Geofence boundaries:
- Are approximate and may have accuracy variations
- May trigger false entry/exit alerts due to GPS drift
- May not trigger alerts if GPS signal is lost
- Cannot account for vertical position (e.g., different floors in a building)

### 7.4 Location History

- Location history may have gaps due to device being offline
- Historical location data is not guaranteed to be complete or accurate
- Location tracking frequency may vary based on battery-saving settings

### 7.5 No Guarantee of Location Accuracy

**The Company is NOT liable for:**
- Inaccurate location information
- Failure to detect geofence violations
- Inability to locate a person in an emergency
- Lost or delayed location updates
